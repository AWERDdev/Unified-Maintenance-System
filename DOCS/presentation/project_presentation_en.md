Project Name: Unified Maintenance Ticket System for Schools.

The Problem: Schools constantly face hardware and infrastructure breakdowns (like smartboards, computers, electricity, and plumbing).

Current Situation: Requesting repairs requires endless paperwork and long administrative cycles. This wastes massive time while broken assets disrupt classrooms.

The Solution: A fast, digital platform that allows schools to log any defect within seconds, routing the issue directly to management for immediate action.

💡 Potential Judge Question:
"If multiple schools submit urgent maintenance requests at the exact same time, how does the system decide which school gets priority?"

2. How it Works & Features
Auto-Context Recognition: The system automatically identifies the specific school upon login, preventing redundant data entry.

Fast Reporting: Filing a ticket takes less than a minute—just input the affected asset, location, estimated cost, and category.

Full Bilingual Support: Complete English and Arabic integration (with proper alignment) to suit all users.

Smart Categorization: Automatically groups logs into Infrastructure, Hardware, Electrical, or Plumbing to help dispatch the right repair technicians instantly.

💡 Potential Judge Question:
"How do you ensure that user-entered data—like the estimated cost or the room location—is accurate and free from human error or exaggeration?"

3. Impact & Future Goals
Time Efficiency: Slashing maintenance response time from several weeks to just a few hours or days.

Asset Preservation: Fixing minor structural issues early before they escalate into massive, expensive damages.

Uninterrupted Learning: Making sure labs and lectures never stall due to broken screens or technical failures.

Next Steps: Expanding the system into a central tracking dashboard for ministry officials to monitor repair completion rates across all districts.

💡 Potential Judge Question:
"How can this system move from just reacting to broken equipment, to helping schools perform preventative maintenance before a breakdown even happens?"
